## üîç Deep Dive by Platform

### 1. Product Hunt (PH)
*   **The "Launch Day"**: On PH, you have **24 hours** to shine. The leaderboard resets every day at 12:01 AM PST.
*   **The "Maker Comment"**: As soon as you post, write a comment introducing yourself.
    *   *Template*: "Hi Hunters! üëã I'm [Name]. I built The Map of Science because..."
*   **Images**: You need a 240x240 logo and a few 1270x760 gallery images. **GIFs work best**.

### 2. Hacker News (HN)
*   **The "Show HN" Tag**: Your title MUST start with `Show HN:`.
    *   *Good*: `Show HN: The Map of Science ‚Äì Interactive visualization of knowledge`
    *   *Bad*: `The Map of Science is the best way to learn!`
*   **The First Comment**: Explain the technical stack (Neo4j + React). HN users love implementation details.
*   **Brutal Honesty**: Expect critical feedback. Don't take it personally; they critique the code/idea, not you.

### 3. Reddit
*   **Subreddit Culture**:
    *   `r/reactjs`: Show your code, talk about performance (e.g., "How I rendered 10k nodes").
    *   `r/dataisbeautiful`: **Must** include a comment with "Source" (where data came from) and "Tools" (what you used to build it). Strict rules on visuals.
    *   `r/webdev`: Focus on the dev journey.
*   **Self-Promotion**: Check the sidebar rules of EACH subreddit before posting. Some only allow "Showoff Saturday".

### 4. Twitter / X
*   **The "Build in Public" Movement**: You don't just launch once. You launch every feature.
    *   *Day 1*: "I added a timeline view!" (Video)
    *   *Day 2*: "Fixed a bug with Neo4j indexing." (Screenshot of code)
*   **Tags**: Use #buildinpublic, #indiehacker, #javascript.
